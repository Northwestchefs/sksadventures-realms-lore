# Sembia

## Overview
Sembia is a commerce-driven nation where political power is closely tied to trade wealth and urban influence.

## Identity
A realm of ledgers, contracts, and ambitious city interests, with reputation often measured in market control.

## Geography
Positioned to benefit from regional overland and maritime commerce; local geography can be expanded for campaign needs.

## Government
Governance is influenced by merchant magnates, civic councils, and legal frameworks designed to protect trade.

## Society
Urban culture prizes negotiation, enterprise, and social mobility through business success.

## Economy
Banking, trade houses, shipping, and contract law form the backbone of Sembian power.

## Religion
Add major temples, patron deities of commerce, and points of friction between faith and finance.

## Military
Defense can include city militias, hired companies, and security forces aligned with major economic actors.

## Major Settlements
- [[settlements/selgaunt|Selgaunt]] (capital)
- Additional trade cities and caravan hubs (TBD)

## Factions and Power Blocs
- Leading merchant families
- Banking circles and investment syndicates
- Civic legal authorities and mercantile courts

## Notable People

## Relations
- Strong economic interdependence with neighbors
- Competitive diplomacy where trade policy doubles as foreign policy

## Threats
- Market crashes and debt crises
- Corporate espionage and sabotage
- External powers manipulating trade dependencies

## Adventure Use
Sembia supports financial intrigue, white-collar crime plots, mercantile diplomacy, and urban political campaigns.

## Hooks
- A failed bank run reveals a forged charter linked to a foreign patron.
- A trade convoy vanishes, threatening a fragile regional treaty.
- The party must arbitrate a contract dispute before it becomes a street war.
