# Baldur's Gate

## Overview
Baldur's Gate is a hard-edged port polity where wealth, crime, and civic authority are in constant negotiation.

## Identity
The city-state is known for ruthless opportunity, entrenched class divides, and institutions that can be both resilient and compromised.

## Geography
Located on a strategic river-sea corridor, Baldur's Gate controls access between inland trade and coastal shipping lanes.

## Government
Formal civic offices share practical power with merchant elites, legal guild interests, and shadow intermediaries.

## Society
Sharp contrasts define daily life: upper-city privilege, lower-city struggle, and outer-district survival economies.

## Economy
Port fees, warehousing, caravan exchange, river traffic, and speculative ventures drive prosperity and predation alike.

## Religion
Multiple faiths operate publicly, often acting as community anchors in districts where civil trust is weak.

## Military
City guards, harbor patrols, and contracted blades enforce order unevenly, often reflecting local patron pressure.

## Major Settlements
- [[settlements/baldurs-gate-city|Baldur's Gate (City)]]: center of law, commerce, and faction competition.

## Factions and Power Blocs
- merchant patriar circles and allied financiers
- [[factions/flaming-fist|Flaming Fist]]
- district-level enforcer networks
- guild coalitions shaping labor and licensing
- faith-backed charities and local militias

## Notable People
- [[people/marshal-saelra-vonn|Marshal Saelra Vonn]]

## Relations
Baldur's Gate maintains pragmatic ties with nearby powers, prioritizing route control, revenue protection, and political autonomy.

## Threats
- corruption in customs and judicial channels
- gang escalation tied to elite sponsorship
- food and fuel shocks in poorer districts
- covert pressure from rival ports and external agents

## Adventure Use
Ideal for street-to-court intrigue, anti-corruption campaigns, smuggling investigations, and faction-brokered peacekeeping.

## Hooks
- A seized cargo manifest names patrons from both lawful and criminal factions.
- A district relief effort is sabotaged to trigger unrest before a trade summit.
- The party is hired to protect a magistrate who may expose a revenue-fraud coalition.
