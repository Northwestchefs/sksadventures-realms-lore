# Amn

## Overview
Amn is a trade-first coastal power where contracts, shipping access, and financial leverage often matter more than hereditary honor. It works best in play as a realm of deals, pressure, and deniable enforcement.

## Identity
- public self-image: practical, orderly, prosperous
- real engine: competing merchant blocs, patronage, and credit control
- table truth: in Amn, ideology often loses to leverage

## Geography and Borders
Amn anchors the southwest trade web between [[nations-realms/tethyr|Tethyr]], [[nations-realms/calimshan|Calimshan]], and interior caravan routes of the [[regions/western-heartlands|Western Heartlands]].

Key connective areas:
- [[regions/amnian-coast|Amnian Coast]] for ports and maritime customs power
- [[regions/trade-way|Trade Way]] for caravan flow and toll politics
- border pressure zones near the [[nations-realms/border-kingdoms|Border Kingdoms]]

## Government and Power
The state is formally led by the [[factions/council-of-six|Council of Six]], but practical authority is distributed through:
- merchant dynasties and investment syndicates
- guild alliances and labor-control networks
- port officials, magistrates, and tax agents tied to patrons

## Economy
Amn's economy runs on movement and credit:
- port duties, warehousing, ship finance, and insurance-like risk pools
- caravan brokerage and toll infrastructure
- aggressive lending, debt leverage, and contract law
- persistent smuggling and tariff evasion woven into legal trade

## Faith and Civic Religion
Amn is pluralistic in worship but transactional in policy. Faiths that stabilize trade, labor, and civic trust gain outsized influence.

Commonly visible in Amnian play:
- [[faiths/waukeen|Waukeen]] (commerce, deals, market legitimacy)
- [[faiths/helm|Helm]] (guard duty, civic order, caravan protection)

## Military and Security
Amn uses mixed security layers:
- regular forces for borders and major infrastructure
- contract troops and private marines tied to merchant interests
- urban enforcement split among official and semi-private actors

## Relationships
Amn's diplomacy is primarily economic. It pressures rivals through tariffs, access rights, convoy protection, and credit terms before open war.

## Canon Notes
Specific rulers, offices, and local control patterns vary by edition and era. Present Council-era oligarchy as stable baseline, then tune details to your campaign year.
