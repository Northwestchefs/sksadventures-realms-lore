# Narfell

## Overview
Narfell is treated here as a nation-scale realm entry for campaign planning, diplomacy, and map-level play.

## Identity
A frontier successor region with distinct identity and recurring strategic significance in the east.

## Geography
Cold plains and rough uplands north of the Inner Sea sphere, near [[nations-realms/vaasa|Vaasa]] and [[nations-realms/great-dale|Great Dale]].

## Government
Usually fragmented among warbands, local rulers, and temporary confederations.

## Society
Clan memory, martial reputation, and survival ethics guide social structure.

## Economy
Pastoralism, hunting, caravan interception, and frontier trade coexist unevenly.

## Religion
Religious life can be volatile, mixing local traditions with imported cult influences.

## Military
Fast-moving warbands and hardened frontier fighters are common military forms.

## Major Settlements
- [[settlements/index|Dun-Tharos]] (detail page TBD)
- [[settlements/index|Narfell March Camps]] (detail page TBD)

## Factions and Power Blocs
- Clan chiefs and war leaders
- Occult or cultic circles
- Mercenary bands serving foreign patrons

## Relations
Operates as both threat and buffer for [[nations-realms/great-dale|Great Dale]], [[nations-realms/vaasa|Vaasa]], and eastern trade states.

## Threats
- Warlord unification under expansionist agendas
- Cult resurgence and demon-bound pacts
- Humanitarian collapse after harsh winters

## Adventure Use
Use Narfell for border diplomacy, faction bargaining, travel logistics, and region-level crisis arcs.

## Hooks
- A disputed treaty clause pulls the party into negotiations between local power blocs and foreign envoys.
- A key route node goes silent, and multiple factions race to control what happened there.
- A local authority hires neutral troubleshooters before a political summit turns violent.
