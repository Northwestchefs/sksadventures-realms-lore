# Chult

## Overview
Chult is a peninsula-scale realm defined by maritime trade nodes, difficult interior terrain, and layered local traditions.

## Identity
In many campaigns, Chult is best treated as a frontier of opportunity and danger where practical alliances matter more than distant claims of authority.

## Geography
Dense jungle, river systems, uplands, and hazardous ruins shape travel, logistics, and political reach from coastal centers.

## Government
Authority is often strongest in major ports and trade hubs, with interior communities maintaining significant autonomy.

## Society
Chult hosts diverse local cultures, expatriate enclaves, and expedition traffic, creating both collaboration and friction.

## Economy
Port commerce, chartered expeditions, specialty goods, and transport services define the region's external economic role.

## Religion
Religious life varies by settlement and community, with longstanding local practice often overlapping imported faith traditions.

## Military
Security depends on port defenses, expedition escorts, local militias, and negotiated passage arrangements.

## Major Settlements
- [[settlements/port-nyanzaru|Port Nyanzaru]]: principal coastal hub and expedition gateway.

## Factions and Power Blocs
- merchant charter interests
- expedition financiers and logistics brokers
- local leadership councils and militia captains
- shrine networks with regional influence

## Relations
Chult's external ties are often transactional, focused on shipping, supply contracts, and managed access to interior routes.

## Threats
- route collapse from weather, monsters, or sabotage
- exploitation by foreign investors
- conflict between expedition interests and local sovereignty
- ruin-linked hazards destabilizing nearby settlements

## Adventure Use
Excellent for exploration campaigns, contract politics, hazardous logistics, and diplomacy between local and external powers.

## Hooks
- A lucrative charter route fails repeatedly, suggesting deliberate interference.
- Competing patrons each claim legal rights to recover artifacts from the same ruin corridor.
- A coastal council asks neutral outsiders to arbitrate a dispute before it turns violent.
