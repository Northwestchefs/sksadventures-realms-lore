# Rootmarket

## Overview
Callidae's trade quarter, woven through colossal roots where cavern caravans exchange goods, lore, and favors.

## Atmosphere
Warm lantern fungi, dense bargaining crowds, and rope-lifts creaking between root halls create constant motion.

## District Use at the Table
- **Open Trade Hours:** Most stalls run from first glow to deep bell, with peak traffic during caravan arrivals.
- **Quiet Window:** The hour before dawn-bell is ideal for secret deals, informants, and shadow deliveries.
- **Street Pressure:** Porters, shrine-collectors, debt-runners, and curious apprentices keep every path crowded.

## Merchant Quarter Layout
- [[landmarks/many-threads-bazaar|Many-Threads Bazaar]] — Main public market ring for everyday trade and fast bargains.
- [[landmarks/root-coin-exchange|Root-Coin Exchange]] — Assayers, scribes, and bonded lenders handling letters of credit.
- [[landmarks/caravan-rest-of-nine-arches|Caravan Rest of Nine Arches]] — Neutral caravan inn where long-haul deals close.
- [[landmarks/emberlane-spice-coils|Emberlane Spice Coils]] — Heated lanes of spice, fungus oils, teas, and alchemical seasonings.
- [[landmarks/lanternhook-quay|Lanternhook Quay]] — Lift docks where freight roots connect to deep tunnel routes.

## Encounter Seeds
1. A trusted spice caravan arrives with every crate correctly sealed but carrying the wrong scent-signatures.
2. A debt ledger from the Root-Coin Exchange names a dead noble-house as guarantor for half the quarter.
3. Rope-lifts at Lanternhook Quay stop in unison for exactly one minute every night, then restart with missing cargo.
4. A disguised noble offers the party a fortune to sabotage a rival's bid at the Nine Arches without spilling blood.
5. Kaelor's broker network goes silent after someone starts buying every map that includes abandoned tunnels.
6. Street preachers claim a sentient root beneath the bazaar is choosing which merchants prosper and which fail.
